Scientific Research

The Science Behind Microwave Pyrolysis

Our microwave pyrolysis technology is backed by extensive peer-reviewed research from leading institutions worldwide. The process uses microwave energy to heat plastic waste in an oxygen-free environment, causing thermal depolymerization that breaks long-chain polymers into valuable lighter hydrocarbons. Unlike conventional pyrolysis, microwave heating provides rapid, volumetric heating with precise temperature control, resulting in higher yields of useful products and minimal unwanted byproducts. Research confirms this approach achieves superior energy efficiency while eliminating harmful emissions typical of conventional plastic recycling methods.
